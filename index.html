Time series of body volume and MOCI anomalies

The figure below shows annual standardized anomalies in adult euphausiid body volume and the central CCE Multivariate Ocean Climate Indicator (MOCI) from 2005–2025 for E. pacifica, T. spinifera, and N. difficilis. Colored bars represent annual body volume anomalies, calculated relative to the long-term mean body volume for each species, while the black line and points represent annual MOCI anomalies relative to the long-term mean MOCI. Positive anomalies indicate values above the long-term average, and negative anomalies indicate values below the average.

Juvenile body volume response to ocean climate

The figure below shows generalized additive model (GAM) predictions for the relationship between MOCI and juvenile body volume in E. pacifica, T. spinifera, and N. difficilis. Lines represent the predicted log-transformed body volume, and shaded ribbons show 95% confidence intervals. Models were weighted by the number of individuals measured per sample.

Adult body volume response to ocean climate

The figure below shows GAM predictions for the relationship between MOCI and adult euphausiid body volume by sex in the three dominant species. Lines represent predicted log-transformed body volume for females and males, and shaded ribbons show approximate 95% confidence intervals. Models were weighted by the number of individuals measured per sample.

Visualization of adult body volume and environmental variability

The interactive plots below show cruise-specific adult euphausiid body volume for E. pacifica, T. spinifera, and N. difficilis alongside multiple environmental indices and their monthly lags. Bars represent mean adult body volume for each cruise, lines show scaled environmental variables. Each index can be toggled on and off through the interactive legend to show visual comparison of body volume patterns with oceanographic and climate variability across cruises.
